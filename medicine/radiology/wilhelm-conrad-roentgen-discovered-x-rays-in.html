Wilhelm Conrad Roentgen discovered x-rays in

(A) 1859

(B) 1857

(C) 1995

(D) ** 1895

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -W.C. Röntgen reported the discovery of X-rays in December 1895 after seven weeks of assiduous work during which he had studied the properties of this new type of radiation able to go through screens of notable thickness. He named them X-rays to underline the fact that their nature was unknown.

Concept note-2: -The roentgenogram is named after German physicist Wilhelm Conrad Röntgen, who discovered X-rays in 1895.

Concept note-3: -On 8 November 1895, Röentgen first observed the phenomenon that he named x rays.

Concept note-4: -Roentgen quickly found that X-rays would pass through human tissue too, rendering the bones and tissue beneath visible. News of his discovery spread worldwide, and within a year, doctors in Europe and the United States were using X-rays to locate gun shots, bone fractures, kidney stones and swallowed objects.

Concept note-5: -Few scientific breakthroughs have had as immediate an impact as Wilhelm Conrad Roentgen’s discovery of X-rays, a momentous event that instantly revolutionized the fields of physics and medicine.
