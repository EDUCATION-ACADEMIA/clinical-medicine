when describing the long axis of a tooth when referring to the paralleling technique, which of the following statements is TRUE?

(A) ** the long axis of the tooth divides it into equal halves

(B) the long axis of the oath divides it into equal thirds

(C) the long axis can be seen on each tooth

(D) the long axis of the tooth divides it into equal sections, one section contains only the crown of the tooth, and the other section contains only the root pf the tooth

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -The paralleling technique is the most commonly used technique for exposing periapical and bitewing radiographs because it creates the most accurate representation of a tooth image. It refers to the receptor being positioned parallel to the full length (long axis) of the tooth being radiographed.

Concept note-2: -Which describes the relationship between the receptor and the long axis of the tooth in the paralleling technique? b. The receptor is placed away from the tooth and toward the middle of the oral cavity.

Concept note-3: -The parallel technique is defined as the parallelism between the object to be studied and the plane of the film while the central axis of the X-ray is perpendicularly adjusted to both planes. Unfortunately, it is much easier to get a perpendicular position of the X-ray central axis to the film than to the object.
