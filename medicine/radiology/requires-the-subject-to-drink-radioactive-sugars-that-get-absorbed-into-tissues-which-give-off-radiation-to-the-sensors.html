Requires the subject to drink radioactive sugars that get absorbed into tissues which give off radiation to the sensors.

(A) MRI

(B) X-Ray

(C) CT

(D) ** PET

(E) Ultrasonography

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -The main difference between SPECT and PET scans is the type of radiotracers used. While SPECT scans measure gamma rays, the decay of the radiotracers used with PET scans produce small particles called positrons. A positron is a particle with roughly the same mass as an electron but oppositely charged.

Concept note-2: -PET-CT scans do carry a risk of radiation. This type of scan uses some radiation from x-rays, the substance used in the PET scan, or both. Scanning a smaller body area means less radiation.

Concept note-3: -A radioactive form of glucose (sugar) often used during a positive emission tomography (PET) scan, a type of imaging test. In PET, a small amount of radioactive glucose is injected into a vein, and a scanner makes a picture of where the glucose is being used in the body.
