____ results when the receptors is exposed twice, and two images appear superimposed onto each other

(A) miscellaneous errors

(B) over exposed image

(C) ** double exposure

(D) under exposed image

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Double exposure results when the receptor is exposed twice, and two images appear superimposed onto each other. This error results in two errors; one receptor that was double-exposed and another that was not exposed at all. It is extremely important to avoid this error because it exposes the patient to radiation twice.

Concept note-2: -Double Exposure Definition Double exposure is a technique that combines two different exposures or images that are layered on top of each other. The image overlaid is less than full opacity so a bit of both images can be seen producing an almost ghost-like image.

Concept note-3: -Overexposed images will have a distinct lack of quantum mottle while appearing ‘saturated’ or in extreme cases ‘burnt out’ whereby anatomy is completely obliterated from the radiograph.

Concept note-4: -One of the most common errors when exposing bitewing images is failing to prevent horizontal overlapping. Horizontal overlap is a result of the X-ray beam not passing through the open interproximal area at right angles to a properly positioned detector.

Concept note-5: -Foreshortening of Image: Cause of Foreshortening: Due to excessive vertical angulation (too high) of the x-ray tube during taking the radiograph. This can make it difficult in certain cases like Endodontic treatment where the working length cannot be properly determined due to the difference in size of the tooth.
