Sarah has a chest x-ray that shows she has tubercles in her lungs. What does that mean?

(A) She had tuberculosis in the past but has recovered.

(B) ** She has a dormant form of TB that could become active if her resistance is lowered.

(C) She has an active case of tuberculosis and is being treated with antibiotics.

(D) She has a history of an infection that is similar to tuberculosis and therefore is immune to TB.

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -tuberculosis. A posterior-anterior chest radiograph is used to detect chest abnormalities. Lesions may appear anywhere in the lungs and may differ in size, shape, density, and cavitation. These abnormalities may suggest TB, but cannot be used to definitively diagnose TB.

Concept note-2: -Abnormalities on chest radiographs may be suggestive of, but are never diagnostic of, TB. However, if a person has a positive response to the tuberculin skin test and no symptoms of the disease, chest radiographs can be used to rule out the possibility of pulmonary TB.
