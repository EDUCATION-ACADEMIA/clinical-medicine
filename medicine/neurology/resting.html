Resting

(A) Screaming

(B) ** Take a rest

(C) years

(D) Kill yourself

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -The word rest can be used as both a noun and a verb. But since it cannot be counted, we don’t use a rest. Hence, the expression I am taking a rest is incorrect. Rather, I am taking rest is preferred.

Concept note-2: -“A rest” is a noun. It means a pause from working. The idiom that we use with the noun is, “to take a rest.” This is a parallel structure to some other common idioms, “to take a break, ‘’ “to take a nap, ‘’ and “to take a bath.” In the UK, they say, “to have a bath” instead of “to take a bath.”

Concept note-3: -: to stop doing something for a short period of time : to rest.

Concept note-4: -You should say “get some rest” or “take a break” instead of “take a good rest."
