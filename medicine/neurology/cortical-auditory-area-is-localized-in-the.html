Cortical auditory area is localized in the:

(A) ** temporal gyrus

(B) central sulcus

(C) occipital lobe

(D) frontal gyrus

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -The primary auditory cortex (A1) is located on the superior temporal gyrus in the temporal lobe and receives point-to-point input from the ventral division of the medial geniculate complex; thus, it contains a precise tonotopic map.

Concept note-2: -Superior temporal gyrus (STG) is the site of auditory association cortex (and a site of multisensory integration) and thus necessarily plays some role in spoken word recognition. Evidence that its role extends beyond that of higher-level perceptual processing was found as early as 1871 by Karl Wernicke.

Concept note-3: -Anatomy and Physiology, Systems Auditory cortex in the superior temporal lobe consists of three major subdivisions, which are apparent cytoarchitectonically and histochemically: core, belt (which surrounds the core), and parabelt (PB), all of which contain several subfields.

Concept note-4: -The auditory cortex is the part of the temporal lobe that processes auditory information in humans and many other vertebrates. It is a part of the auditory system, performing basic and higher functions in hearing, such as possible relations to language switching.

Concept note-5: -The primary auditory cortex (Brodmann area 41, 42) is the part of the temporal lobe that is responsible for the conscious perception of sound. It is located in Heschlâ€™s gyrus. It receives inputs from the medial geniculate nucleus.
