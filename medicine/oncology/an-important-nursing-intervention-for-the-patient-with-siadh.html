An important nursing intervention for the patient with SIADH

(A) ** Bed rest.

(B) ** Restrict of fluids.

(C) A low sodium diet.

(D) Safety and seizure precautions.

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -You will restrict fluids and replace sodium as ordered by the provider. Monitor for fluid volume excess. If the patient has too much fluid volume overload, that can lead to pulmonary edema which is life threatening, so itâ€™s important to monitor for that.

Concept note-2: -Fluid restriction is a priority in the management of SIADH, as the main issue with this disease is fluid excess and hyponatremia.

Concept note-3: -Fluid restriction is the main stay of treatment, especially in SIADH. Fluid restriction to less than the urine output is the primary therapy in hyponatremia. Usual recommended fluid intake is less than 800 mg/day.

Concept note-4: -The most commonly prescribed treatment for SIADH is fluid and water restriction. If the condition is chronic, fluid restriction may need to be permanent. Treatment may also include: Certain medications that inhibit the action of ADH (also called vasopressin)

Concept note-5: -The Nursing Process Hyponatremia management involves fluid replacement using sodium-containing fluids, increased oral intake, and other salt-replacing medications.
