Which pathogen has mitochondria?

(A) ** Plasmodium falciparum

(B) Vibrio cholera

(C) HIV

(D) Mycobacterium tuberculosis

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -The phylum Apicomplexa contains a group of protozoa causing diseases in humans and livestock. Plasmodium spp., the causative agent of malaria, contains a mitochondrion that is very divergent from that of their hosts.

Concept note-2: -ABSTRACT. Plasmodium species have a single mitochondrion that is essential for their survival and has been successfully targeted by antimalarial drugs.

Concept note-3: -Malaria Parasites. Malaria parasites are micro-organisms that belong to the genus Plasmodium. There are more than 100 species of Plasmodium, which can infect many animal species such as reptiles, birds, and various mammals.

Concept note-4: -The mitochondrial genome of malaria parasites. Malaria parasites possess a mitochondrial genome in the form of circular and/or tandemly repeated linear elements of 6 kb, the smallest in size among eukaryotic cells [58].

Concept note-5: -falciparum, which exhibits minimal redundancy of tRNAs. The mitochondrial genome of Plasmodium is small (about 6 kb) and encodes no tRNAs, so the mitochondrion must import tRNAs21, 22.
