You should always use soap when washing your hands

(A) ** true

(B) false

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -False. It’s better to use soap and water. Not only does soap help remove soil and germs, but it’s been shown people scrub their hands more thoroughly when using soap.

Concept note-2: -Washing hands with soap and water is the best way to get rid of germs in most situations. If soap and water are not readily available, you can use an alcohol-based hand sanitizer that contains at least 60% alcohol.

Concept note-3: -Washing hands can keep you healthy and prevent the spread of respiratory and diarrheal infections. Germs can spread from person to person or from surfaces to people when you: Touch your eyes, nose, and mouth with unwashed hands.

Concept note-4: -You should always wash your hands with hot water. You can never wash too long or scrub too hard. Letting your hands air dry is a good substitute for towels. You don’t have to dry your hands after washing. Always use antibacterial soap when washing hands. Hand sanitizers can replace handwashing. More items

Concept note-5: -Wet your hands with clean running water (warm or cold). Lather up your hands with soap. Rinse your hands well under running water. Turn off the water with your elbow. Dry your hands with a clean towel or air dry them. More items •03-May-2020
