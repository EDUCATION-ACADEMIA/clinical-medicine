What is a good example or analogy to use for host cells?

(A) ** a human cell being attacked and intruded by influenza

(B) the COVID-19 vaccine being administered to patients

(C) an infected tick latching itself to and feeding off of a praying mantis

(D) Febreze!

(E) a wounded dog barking at a bird’s nest

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -The influenza virus’s hemagglutinin (HA) surface proteins then bind to the sialic acid receptors on the surface of a human respiratory tract cell. The structure of the influenza virus’s HA surface proteins is designed to fit the sialic acid receptors of the human cell, like a key to a lock.

Concept note-2: -The replication of the influenza genome involves two steps: transcription of complimentary RNA (cRNA), followed by transcription of new vRNA copies using the cRNAs as templates.

Concept note-3: -Influenza virus infects respiratory epithelial cells that line the upper (including nasal) through lower (to the alveoli) respiratory tract.

Concept note-4: -T cells and B cells play key roles in adaptive immunity against the IAV infection. T cells are mainly known as CD4+ T and CD8+ T cells. CD8+ T cells differentiate into cytotoxic T lymphocytes (CTLs), which produce cytokines and effector molecules to restrict viral replication and kill virus-infected cells.
