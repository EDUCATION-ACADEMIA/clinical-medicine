What organism causes blight in tomatoes and potatoes?

(A) Bacteria

(B) Virus

(C) Fungus

(D) ** Protoctistan

(E) Parasite

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Phytophthora infestans belongs to a group of oomycetes within the Kingdom of “protists”, although they are still commonly referred to as “fungi”. They are also called “water molds” because they thrive and produce spores under humid, moist environment and cause infection only when free water is present on plant surface.

Concept note-2: -Potato blight (also known as late blight) is a disease caused by a fungus-like (Oomycete) organism (Phytophthora infestans) that spreads rapidly through the foliage and tubers of potatoes in warm, wet weather, causing collapse and decay.

Concept note-3: -Tomato late blight is caused by the oomycete pathogen Phytophthora infestans (P. infestans). The pathogen is best known for causing the devastating Irish potato famine of the 1840s, which killed over a million people, and caused another million to leave the country.

Concept note-4: -late blight, also called potato blight, disease of potato and tomato plants that is caused by the water mold Phytophthora infestans. The disease occurs in humid regions with temperatures ranging between 4 and 29 °C (40 and 80 °F).
