It is very common for someone to have chicken pox 3 or 4 times

(A) true

(B) ** false

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -It’s possible to get chickenpox more than once, but it’s unusual.

Concept note-2: -Most people who have had chickenpox will be immune to the disease for the rest of their lives. However, the virus remains inactive in nerve tissue and may reactivate later in life causing shingles. Very rarely, a second case of chickenpox does happen.

Concept note-3: -It’s highly unlikely that you’ll have chickenpox more than once. And it’s very unusual for people who have had the chickenpox vaccine to contract the virus. If you think that you or your child has contracted the virus, visit your doctor.

Concept note-4: -For most people, getting chickenpox once provides immunity for life. It is possible to get chickenpox more than once, but this is not common.
