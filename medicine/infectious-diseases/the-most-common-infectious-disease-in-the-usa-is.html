The most common infectious disease in the U.S.A. is

(A) flu

(B) whooping cough

(C) ** common cold

(D) strep throat

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Common Cold. The common cold sounds minor, but it may not feel that way. This viral infection of your nose and throat can cause a runny or stuffy nose, cough, congestion, and that general run-down feeling. The virus can live in the air and get in your body through your mouth, eyes, or nose.

Concept note-2: -The common cold is a viral infectious disease that affects the upper respiratory system. It is the most common infectious disease among humans. Most colds result from coronaviruses or rhinoviruses.

Concept note-3: -The viruses that cause colds are very contagious. They can spread from person to person through the air and close personal contact. You can also get infected when you touch something that has the virus on it and then touch your eyes, mouth, or nose.

Concept note-4: -Many different respiratory viruses can cause the common cold, but rhinoviruses are the most common. Rhinoviruses can also trigger asthma attacks and have been linked to sinus and ear infections.

Concept note-5: -Common cold. The flu (influenza). COVID-19. Stomach flu (gastroenteritis). Hepatitis. Respiratory syncytial virus (RSV). 06-Jun-2022
