Which following vessel is not often belong to the ‘left coronary artery system’?

(A) septal branch

(B) ** posterior descending artery

(C) diagonal branch

(D) obtuse margin branch

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -The left coronary artery continues into the superior aspect of the anterior interventricular groove where it bifurcates into its two terminal branches; the left anterior descending artery (anterior interventricular artery) and the circumflex artery.

Concept note-2: -Those most often bypassed today include the right coronary artery, the posterior descending coronary artery, the left main coronary artery, the left anterior descending coronary artery and the left circumflex coronary artery.
