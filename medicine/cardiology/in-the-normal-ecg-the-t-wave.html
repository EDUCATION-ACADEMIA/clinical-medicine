In the normal ECG, the T wave:

(A) It is always positive in DI, DII.V3-V6

(B) ** It is always negative in AVR

(C) ** It can be positive or negative in V1-V2, II and AVF

(D) ** Represents ventricular repolarization

(E) about 1/3 of the R wave

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Ventricular repolarization is the process by which the ventricular myocytes return to their negative resting potential so they can depolarize again. While this phase of the cardiac cycle is rapid, an upright low amplitude broad hump following the QRS complex is seen in normal T wave morphology.

Concept note-2: -T wave. T waves represent ventricular repolarization (atrial repolarization is obscured by the large QRS complex).

Concept note-3: -The T-wave represents the return of the ventricles from excited to normal state (repolarisation).

Concept note-4: -Ventricular repolarization is a complex electrical phenomenon which represents a crucial stage in electrical cardiac activity. It is expressed on the surface electrocardiogram by the interval between the start of the QRS complex and the end of the T wave or U wave (QT).

Concept note-5: -The T wave represents ventricular repolarization. The T wave exhibits a positive deflection. The reason for this is that the last cells to depolarize in the ventricles are the first to repolarize.
