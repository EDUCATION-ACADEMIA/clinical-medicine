A “run” of ventricular tachycardia occurs if at least ____ PVCs occur in a row.

(A) two

(B) ** three

(C) four

(D) five

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -V-tach occurs when your pulse rate is more than 100 beats per minute, and you have at least three irregular heartbeats, or arrhythmias, in a row.

Concept note-2: -It is important to note that three or more consecutive PVCs are classified as ventricular tachycardia. If the PVCs continuously alternate with a regular sinus beat, the patient is in bigeminy. [3] Likewise, if every third heartbeat is a PVC, then it is named trigeminy.

Concept note-3: -When PVC happens in a pattern of three beats, doctors call it trigeminy. It can feel like fluttering in your chest or a skipped heartbeat. There are two kinds of trigeminy: Two normal heartbeats followed by one extra beat.

Concept note-4: -Ventricular Tachycardia exists when three or more PVCs occur in a row at a rate greater than 100 BPM. It is unsustained if it lasts less than 30 seconds.

Concept note-5: -Sustained ventricular tachycardia is defined as tachycardia that continues for more than 30 seconds or leads to hemodynamic compromise within 30 seconds and requires intervention. On the other hand, non-sustained ventricular tachycardia lasts less than 30 seconds and does not cause hemodynamic instability.
