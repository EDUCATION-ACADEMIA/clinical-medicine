Each upper chamber of the heart is called a(n) ____

(A) ventricle

(B) pulmonary artery

(C) spleen

(D) ** atrium

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -The upper chambers-the right and left atria-receive incoming blood. The lower chambers-the right and left ventricles-pump blood out of your heart.

Concept note-2: -The atrium was formerly called the ‘auricle’. That term is still used to describe this chamber in some other animals, such as the Mollusca.

Concept note-3: -atrium, in vertebrates and the higher invertebrates, heart chamber that receives blood into the heart and drives it into a ventricle, or chamber, for pumping blood away from the heart.

Concept note-4: -The upper chamber is called an atrium (or auricle), and the lower chamber is called a ventricle.
